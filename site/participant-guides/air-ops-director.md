# Air Ops Branch Director

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Role** | Air Operations Branch Director (per [ICS-203](../docs/ics-203.md) §7) |
| **Assignee** | **CPT Michael S. Borrilez** (2 BN CO, MRC-2) |
| **Reports to** | Operations Section Chief — LTC Sheaf |
| **Section** | Operations |
| **Operational period** | 13-15 MAY 26 |

## What this role is

The Air Ops Branch Director commands the sUAS element of the exercise. The role:

- **Supervises the three Part 107 pilots** (CPT Widner, 1LT Riley, SFC Acosta from SDFF)
- **Coordinates flight ops** — when, where, what altitude, what camera/sensor posture
- **Owns the sUAS-detection event** on 14 MAY 1400-1430 — this is the primary HAAP training objective per Mr. Armstrong (20 APR)
- **Coordinates with HAAP detection systems** through the IC / Liaison
- **Stays inside the [JAG UAS Memo](../docs/jag-uas-memo.md) constraints** — Part 107 compliance, no imagery within HAAP fence, no critical-infrastructure imaging (TCA § 39-13-903)

## Expectations during TNMAN-26

### 13 MAY

- On-site at AFRC by **0600**.
- Receive **HAAP POC briefing** alongside the IC if airspace coordination is required.
- Brief the three pilots on:
  - Flight zone — the **blue oval on slide 4** of [Ops Graphics](../docs/ops-graphics.md)
  - **NO-GO Line** — do not cross
  - **No imagery within the HAAP fence** (LTC Sheaf 11 APR; TCA § 39-13-903)
  - Detection-event focus: HAAP detection system is the audience
  - Flight log requirements (ICS-214 + Part 107 records)
- Verify pilot certifications and aircraft airworthiness.

### 14 MAY

- **Continuous presence at the Air Ops position** — likely co-located with or near the ICP staging area, with line-of-sight to the flight zone.
- **Run the 1400-1430 sUAS detection event**:
  - Brief pilots on flight pattern
  - Coordinate launch / land sequencing
  - Maintain comms with HAAP detection system through the IC / Liaison
  - **Observe and document detection results** for the AAR
- Coordinate with **SECFOR Branch (MAJ Crosby)** on observation overlap — what the foot patrol sees vs. what Air Ops flies.
- Brief the **Ops Section Chief** at each transition.

### 15 MAY

- Capture Air-Ops-specific AAR — what worked in the detection event, what didn't, recommendations for future TNMAN.

## Rank vs role

CPT is appropriate for a Branch Director on this TF. **No internal rank-vs-role tension within the Air Ops Branch.** Several related dynamics:

- **You are dual-hatted as 2 BN CO** — primary duty is the Air Ops Branch on this exercise; 2 BN CO authority is contextually backgrounded.
- **One of your pilots (CPT Widner) is your peer in rank** — a CPT under a CPT in the ICS structure. The role distinction (Branch Director vs Pilot) governs; rank is irrelevant within the assignment.
- **Another pilot is 1LT Riley** (3 BN XO) — a junior officer reporting to you within the Branch.
- **The third pilot is SFC Acosta** (TNSG IRR / SDFF) — an NCO from an adjacent organization, integrated under the IAP. He's also the **SDFF Agency Representative** in [ICS-203 §4](../docs/ics-203.md), which gives him a separate liaison line to the IC outside the Air Ops Branch chain.
- **The other Branch Director is MAJ Crosby (SECFOR)** — peer-rank as Branch Director, senior in absolute rank. Coordinate horizontally; route through the Ops Chief for any cross-Branch dispute.

## Out of scope (do NOT do)

- **Do not direct SECFOR positioning** — that's MAJ Crosby.
- **Do not authorize flights beyond the blue-oval zone** or across the NO-GO Line.
- **Do not authorize imagery within the HAAP fence** — JAG memo and HAAP guidance both prohibit.
- **Do not engage on personally-owned DJI airframes** without resolution of the [DJI Pre-Brief](../docs/jag-brief-dji.md) — JAG response is pending. Coordinate with LTC Sheaf and the IC if any pilot brings a DJI.
- **Do not act as Safety Officer** — coordinate with SFC Sturgill.
