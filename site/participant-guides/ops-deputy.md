# Operations Section Deputy

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Role** | Operations Section Deputy (per [ICS-203](../docs/ics-203.md) §7) |
| **Assignee** | **1SG Jeffrey S. Collins** (2 BN HQ Co 1SG, MRC-1) |
| **Reports to** | Operations Section Chief — LTC Sheaf |
| **Section** | Operations |
| **Operational period** | 13-15 MAY 26 |

## What this role is

The Operations Deputy supports the Ops Chief in running the section. The role:

- **Backs the Ops Chief** at the command rail; takes the seat when the Chief is briefing the IC, on the phone with DIV, or otherwise pulled
- **Translates** Ops Chief direction into specifics for Branch Directors and the Asst Ops Specialist
- **Tracks the Execution Matrix** in real time — does the actual schedule still match what's happening
- Owns **continuity across transitions** within the operational period (morning brief → execution → AAR)
- Acts as **operational battle captain** alongside the Ops Chief during inject windows

## Expectations during TNMAN-26

### 13 MAY

- On-site by **0600** with the Ops Chief.
- Walk the **ICP IOC sequence** with Sheaf — physically check that staging, SECFOR position, Air Ops, and Comms are coming up on schedule.
- Coordinate with the **Asst Ops Specialist (SGT Malone)** — SGT Malone is MRC-3 single-day on 14 MAY, so the Deputy may also need to cover Asst-Ops functions on 13 and 15 MAY.

### 14 MAY

- **Cover the rail** when LTC Sheaf is briefing the IC, briefing DIV, or stepping out.
- **Track the Execution Matrix** through inject windows; flag delays / shortfalls to the Ops Chief.
- Coordinate with **Branch Directors** (Crosby for SECFOR, Borrilez for Air Ops, Rookard for Staging) on inject readiness 15 minutes ahead of each timed inject.
- Maintain a **rolling personal ICS-214** that captures Operations Section activity (what was tasked, when it executed, what it produced).

### 15 MAY

- Cover the rail during the AAR cycle.
- Hand off section ICS-214 contents to the **Documentation Unit (CPT McCroskey)**.

## Rank vs role

This is a clear rank-vs-role example. **1SG is a senior NCO; the Operations Section Chief is an LTC.** A 1SG as Deputy under an LTC Section Chief is a common ICS arrangement, and on this exercise it's the right call given who's available — but the dynamic is worth being explicit about.

- **Authority within the Operations Section runs through the role.** When 1SG Collins covers the rail in LTC Sheaf's absence, his operational direction has the weight of the Section, not of his rank insignia.
- **Branch Directors include CPT Borrilez and MAJ Crosby** — both organically outrank 1SG Collins. When the Deputy is acting as battle captain, those Branch Directors take operational direction through the Ops Section seat regardless. Outside the IAP execution chain, they're peers / seniors of Collins.
- **In NCO-channel matters** (standards, mentorship, soldier care), 1SG Collins's rank still carries weight — including with junior officers in the Section.
- **Collins defers to LTC Sheaf** on Section-Chief-level decisions (resource allocation, scope changes, Section AAR direction). When Sheaf is present and engaged, Collins supports.

The same dynamic applies to **SFC Bradley as Logistics Deputy** (under CPT Haddix) and **SFC Ferguson as SECFOR Deputy** (under MAJ Crosby).

## Out of scope (do NOT do)

- **Do not run a Branch.** That's the Branch Director's lane. Deputy stays at the Section level.
- **Do not drive injects.** Exercise control / DIV runs MSEL.
- **Do not commit the TF** on external requests — that's IC / Liaison.
- **Do not duplicate the Asst Ops Specialist's logging** — the Asst Ops captures detail; the Deputy captures decision points.
