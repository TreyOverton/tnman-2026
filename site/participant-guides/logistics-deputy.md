# Logistics Section Deputy

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Role** | Logistics Section Deputy (per [ICS-203](../docs/ics-203.md) §6) |
| **Assignee** | **SFC Timothy W. Bradley** (HHC Rec NCO, MRC-1) |
| **Reports to** | Logistics Section Chief — CPT Haddix |
| **Section** | Logistics |
| **Operational period** | 13-15 MAY 26 |

## What this role is

The Logistics Deputy supports the Logistics Section Chief and runs the physical setup / teardown of the Logistics footprint. The role:

- **Backs CPT Haddix** at the Logistics position; takes the seat when the Chief is briefing the IC, coordinating with the Staging Area, or stepping out
- **Owns physical setup** — tables, tents, signage, charging strip, water containers
- **Coordinates with the Staging Area Manager (MAJ Rookard)** on overlapping setup work
- **Tracks consumables** — water, snacks, batteries, hand-sanitizer, sunscreen, etc.

## Expectations during TNMAN-26

### 13 MAY

- **On-site by 0530** with CPT Haddix.
- **Lead physical setup** — staging tables, sign-in tables, Logistics position, charging strip, water station.
- Coordinate with the **Staging Area Manager** on sign-in flow and POV parking.
- Coordinate with **CSM Rutherford / SSG Singh** on comms equipment placement.
- Coordinate with **MAJ Gampe** on Medical Unit position and casualty-point setup.
- Brief CPT Haddix on physical readiness.

### 14 MAY

- **Continuous presence at the Logistics position.**
- Cover the rail when CPT Haddix is briefing the IC or the Ops Chief.
- Manage **water resupply and consumables rotation** — particularly through the warmest part of the day.
- Coordinate with **Service Branch Director (2LT Neisler)** on charging-strip rotation, vehicle moves, equipment swaps.
- Maintain ICS-214; flag AAR observations.

### 15 MAY

- Coordinate **physical teardown** — Logistics position, charging strip, water station, signage.
- Verify equipment turn-in.

## Rank vs role

Rank-vs-role example, similar to the **Operations Deputy (1SG Collins)** dynamic.

- **SFC reports to CPT Section Chief** — appropriate rank line, normal officer-NCO relationship.
- **The interesting dynamic is upward**: when covering the Logistics rail in CPT Haddix's absence, you direct other elements of the Section, including the **Comms Unit led by CSM Rutherford** (E-9). Direct via the role; rely on CSM Rutherford's expertise; don't override on technical calls.
- **Across the staging area**, you coordinate with **MAJ Rookard (Staging Area Manager)** — a rank-senior coordinating peer, not a subordinate. Coordinate horizontally; don't task him.

## Out of scope (do NOT do)

- **Do not act for the Logistics Chief on Section-direction-level decisions** without clear delegation.
- **Do not run a Unit** — Comms Unit and Medical Unit have their own leadership.
- **Do not freelance setup of the Staging Area** — that's MAJ Rookard's lane; coordinate.
- **Do not duplicate Service Branch Director's task list** — Neisler has hot-spot logistics; you handle physical setup and consumables baseline.
