## What this role is

The PIO controls the unit's external messaging during the exercise. The role:

- Owns **press/media interactions** — though no media activity is expected on a closed exercise, posture matters
- Coordinates with **TNARNG PAO** for any consolidated public products (per CG intent — TNSG PAO consolidates with TNARNG PAO)
- Routes all **external statements** about the exercise (informal photos, social posts, public comments) through approval before release
- Drafts **internal-only photos / message products** if the exercise produces any
- Briefs the **IC** on any media or external interest detected during the operational day

In a TNMAN-style exercise where the operational footprint is largely outside the HAAP fence on US-11W, the PIO's main work is **proactive — making sure no participant accidentally generates a public-facing artifact** from a personal phone or social account.

- Brief at the morning brief on **OPSEC** for the day: no photos within the HAAP fence; no social posts during the operational window; no callsign/frequency capture.

### 14 MAY

- Be on-call for any external interest (passersby, civilian questions on US-11W).
- Field any media query through the IC; do not engage independently.
- Track participant social activity informally — flag concerns to the IC and RCSM.

### 15 MAY

- Capture any public-facing observations / lessons for the AAR.
- Coordinate any post-exercise consolidated artifact with TNARNG PAO.

## Rank vs role

N/A pending fill. Once assigned, the PIO is typically a CPT-MAJ in DIV PAO or a TNARNG E-7+ MOS-37F. The PIO's authority is the role — they may direct any participant on messaging compliance regardless of rank distance.

## Out of scope (do NOT do)

- **Do not engage external agencies** on operational specifics — that's the Liaison Officer or the IC.
- **Do not approve releases without IC concurrence** for anything beyond standard internal photos.
- **Do not become a TF spokesperson** — the IC speaks for the unit; the PIO supports.
