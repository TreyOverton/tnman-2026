## What this role is

The Medical Unit provides **on-site medical support** for the operational period. The role:

- **Owns the casualty point** — first-aid station at or near the ICP, with line-of-sight to staging
- **Triages and stabilizes** any minor injury on-site; coordinates 911 / EMS transfer for anything beyond minor
- **Prophylactic support** per DIV OPORD §4.c.(5) — hydration ('Drip Drop'), sunscreen, insect repellent, mole skin, bandages "as needed"
- **Coordinates with the Safety Officer (SFC Sturgill)** continuously — heat injury, hydration, fatigue, injury cluster
- **Briefs the Logistics Section Chief** at the morning brief on Medical readiness
- **Interfaces with HAAP first-aid** and local EMS as required

The Medical Unit is **3 PAX, all attached from 61st MED Co (31st Med Co, Green/Yellow tab) — DIV-tasked per DIV OPORD §3.b.(1)**.

> **Important constraint:** Per DIV OPORD §4.c.(4), **61st MED Co will NOT provide PHAs during TNMAN.** Personnel arrive with PHAs current; the Medical Unit is operational support, not records support.

## Rank vs role

Two distinct rank dynamics:

- **MAJ Gampe outranks the Logistics Section Chief (CPT Haddix)** — Field Officer reporting to a Captain by ICS assignment. This is a structural quirk of the assignment: the Medical Unit Leader is whoever 61st MED Co sends, and they happened to send a MAJ. Within the IAP, MAJ Gampe takes Section direction from CPT Haddix on Section-level integration; he retains clinical authority within the Medical Unit.
- **The Medical Unit reports through DIV's 61st MED BN chain in parallel** — for clinical issues and tasking outside this exercise, MAJ Gampe routes through 61st MED BN (BG Cox), not through 3 RGT.
- **In NCO-channel and senior-officer matters**, MAJ Gampe carries the weight of his rank — he should be visibly senior in the team for any officer-development or mentorship moment.
- **MAJ Gampe's clinical authority is non-negotiable** — like the Safety Officer's stop-work authority, a Medical Unit clinical call (e.g., "this Soldier is heat-injured and needs to leave") holds regardless of any Section's operational tempo. Back his calls.

## Out of scope (do NOT do)

- **Do not provide PHAs** — explicitly excluded by DIV OPORD §4.c.(4).
- **Do not run Operations or SECFOR tasks** — the Medical Unit operates outside the IAP-execution chain.
- **Do not engage external EMS / hospitals on the unit's behalf** without IC awareness — coordinate the call, but the IC owns the unit-level decision.
- **Do not freelance casualty-point setup** — coordinate with Logistics Deputy.
