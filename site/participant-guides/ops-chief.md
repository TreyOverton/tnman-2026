| **Operational period** | 13-15 MAY 26 |

## What this role is

The Operations Section Chief runs the day-to-day execution of the IAP. While the IC sets intent and accepts risk, the Ops Chief turns intent into actions:

- **Coordinates inject response** across SECFOR, Air Ops, and the Staging Area
- Owns the **operational tempo** — when sections move, when they pause, when they hand off
- **Briefs the IC** on operational status; receives the IC's operational decisions and translates them into Section direction
- **Synchronizes the Operations Section** — Branch Directors report to you; you report to the IC
- Is the **default battle captain** for the ICP during execution

In a SECFOR-and-sUAS exercise like TNMAN, the Ops Chief is the busiest billet in the ICP from 1200 onward on 14 MAY.

## Rank vs role

LTC is the appropriate rank for an Ops Section Chief on a TF this size. **No internal rank-vs-role tension.** Two related dynamics:

- **You are MRC-2** — DD 2977 places you ICP-interior for the operational period. SECFOR Branch Director (MAJ Crosby) executes external coordination at the screen position.
- **Branch Directors include CPT Borrilez (2 BN CO) and MAJ Crosby (3 BN CO)** — both are organic 3 RGT BN COs reporting to you on this exercise. The dynamic is a Section Chief directing peer-rank BN-CO subordinates within the ICS structure. This is normal in ICS; the IAP governs.

## Out of scope (do NOT do)

- **Do not act for the IC** without explicit delegation — that's the Deputy IC (LTC Smith).
- **Do not do JAG / S1 / S6 work** — separate teams.
- **Do not run Planning** — that's 1LT Overton's section. Coordinate with him.
- **Do not run Logistics / Sustain** — that's CPT Haddix.
- **Do not freelance external coordination** — Liaison Officer routes external traffic.
