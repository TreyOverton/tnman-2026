## What this role is

The SECFOR Branch Deputy backs the SECFOR Branch Director and covers the SECFOR billet on days the Director is absent. The role:

- **Acts as Branch Director** on 13 MAY (ICP IOC) and 15 MAY (AAR) — MAJ Crosby is MRC-3 single-day on 14 MAY only
- **Backs the Branch Director** at the SECFOR rail on 14 MAY during inject windows
- **Mentors and runs day-to-day discipline** of the foot patrol element (squad-leader-of-squad-leaders dynamic)
- **Coordinates with the Screen Division/Group Leader (2LT Garrison)** on patrol position, rotation, and notional-patrol tabletop work

## Expectations during TNMAN-26

### 13 MAY (acting as Branch Director)

- On-site by **0600**.
- Coordinate with the **Ops Section Chief** on SECFOR posture for the day — SECFOR is largely in the ICP setting up; physical patrol does not deploy until 14 MAY.
- Walk the **foot patrol team** (SSG Whalen leading; PFC Human; PV2 Elrod) through their 14 MAY plan — position, NO-GO Line, civilian-interaction posture, hydration.
- Verify CalTopo dots are live for SECFOR personnel.
- Prep the **Screen Division/Group tabletop materials** with 2LT Garrison.

### 14 MAY (backing MAJ Crosby)

- **Continuous presence at the SECFOR rail** with MAJ Crosby.
- Cover the rail when Crosby is briefing the Ops Chief or the IC.
- During the **1400-1430 sUAS event**, coordinate with the foot patrol on observation reporting.
- Track **rest / hydration / position rotation** of the foot patrol; flag concerns to the Safety Officer.

### 15 MAY (acting as Branch Director)

- Cover the SECFOR contribution to the AAR.
- Capture SECFOR-specific observations for the Documentation Unit.

## Rank vs role

This is a rank-vs-role example. **SFC is the standard rank for a senior SECFOR NCO; SFC as Branch Deputy under a MAJ Branch Director is a normal arrangement** that doctrinally would have an O-3 or O-4 Deputy in a fully-staffed unit.

- **When acting as Branch Director on 13 and 15 MAY**, your operational direction has the weight of the Branch — even when interacting with peer or senior NCOs in other branches. The IAP and the assignment govern.
- **2LT Garrison (Screen Division/Group Leader) reports to you within the Branch** — a 2LT subordinate to a SFC by role, even though the rank-line normally runs the other direction. This is normal in ICS and increasingly normal in TNSG given staffing realities. Garrison brings junior-officer-track perspective; you bring SECFOR depth and senior-NCO experience. Both work the same problem.
- **Outside the Branch**, the dynamic is normal — you defer to officers in their roles and lean on rank in NCO-channel matters.

## Out of scope (do NOT do)

- **Do not direct Air Ops** — that's CPT Borrilez.
- **Do not commit SECFOR PAX inside the HAAP fence** — perimeter only.
- **Do not override Safety Officer calls** — back SFC Sturgill.
- **Do not freelance external coordination** with HAAP or civilians — route to MAJ Crosby or the Ops Chief.
