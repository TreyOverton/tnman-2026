## What this role is

The Incident Commander commands the Mt. Carmel Site Defense task force. Within the ICS framework, the IC owns the **Incident Action Plan (IAP)** for each operational period and is the single point of accountability for the exercise. Functionally, the IC:

- Sets the **command intent and priority of effort** for each operational period
- **Approves and signs the daily IAP** — the ICS-203 has already been approved (29-30 APR); operational period ICS-202s and follow-on ICS-203s will be signed during execution
- Owns **risk acceptance** — final say on safety calls, scope changes, stop-work authority
- **Interfaces externally** with the HAAP POC, DIV CG, LTC Estes, and any visiting LNOs or distinguished visitors
- **Receives and routes CCIRs** (per [DIV OPORD 26-05](/docs/opord-div.md) §3.f.(2) — FFIRs / PIRs / EEFIs)
- Holds **succession of command** per SOP if commitment requires

The IC role is broader than the TF Commander role in pure ICS doctrine, but for our purposes the two are the same person: COL Roark commands the TNSG TF and runs the ICP.

## Rank vs role

The IC is doctrinally an officer, and COL Roark is the appropriate rank for this task force. **No internal rank-vs-role tension exists for this billet.** A few related dynamics worth keeping in mind:

- **Visiting external officers and senior NCOs may be peers or seniors of organic staff.** LTC Estes is a peer-grade officer and the DIV TNMAN PM; BG Cox would visit. The IC anchors the chain of command on-site — visitors are informed, not in command, unless an explicit transfer is documented.
- **The IC has command authority over LNOs from 2 RGT, 4 RGT, and DIV** while they are operating within the 3 RGT TF — even though those LNOs may carry parent-unit ranks equal to or above some organic staff. Treat the LNOs as integrated team members under the IAP, not as outside observers.
- **Within the ICP, the ICS structure governs functional authority** (e.g., the Safety Officer may stop work regardless of rank). Back the structure unless there's a clear reason not to.

## Out of scope (do NOT do)

- **Do not run Operations day-to-day.** That's the **Operations Section Chief (LTC Sheaf)**. The IC's job is to set intent, accept risk, and unblock — not to coordinate the inject sequence.
- **Do not personally drive injects.** Exercise control / MSEL belongs to DIV (and notional roles within the TF). The IC reacts to injects with the section chiefs.
- **Do not serve as JAG, S1, or S6.** Legal questions route through **COL Williams (DIV PMO)**; personnel goes through the **Resources Unit (1LT Fielitz-Scarbrough)**; comms goes through the **Comms Unit (CSM Rutherford / SSG Singh)**.
- **Do not operate radios, sUAS, or function as SECFOR.**
- **Do not freelance on safety.** Back the **Safety Officer (SFC Sturgill)** — including stop-work calls.
- **Do not personally coordinate with civilian agencies** (TEMA, TDOT, local LE) without going through the **Liaison Officer** or **DIV PAO**.
