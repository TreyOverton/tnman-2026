# Logistics Section Chief

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Role** | Logistics Section Chief (per [ICS-203](/docs/ics-203.md) §6) |
| **Assignee** | **CPT Terrence S. Haddix** (4 BN CMDR, MRC-2) |
| **Reports to** | Incident Commander — COL Roark |
| **Section** | Logistics |
| **Operational period** | 13-15 MAY 26 |

## What this role is

The Logistics Section Chief runs the section that **keeps the team operating** — physical setup, comms, medical, food, water, charging, vehicles. The role:

- **Owns the Logistics Section** — Deputy (SFC Bradley), Service Branch Director (2LT Neisler), Comms Unit (CSM Rutherford / SSG Singh), Medical Unit (MAJ Gampe + 2 medics pending)
- **Coordinates physical setup** of the ICP and staging area on 13 MAY
- **Manages logistics for the screen patrol** — water, snacks, comms, charging
- **Coordinates Medical Unit posture** for the operational period
- **Reports to the IC** on Logistics readiness; coordinates horizontally with Operations and Planning Section Chiefs

## Expectations during TNMAN-26

### 13 MAY

- **On-site by 0530** with SFC Bradley to begin physical setup.
- Coordinate with the **Staging Area Manager (MAJ Rookard)** on staging table layout, charging strip, sign-in flow.
- Verify **Comms Unit setup** — TACN/VHF radios distributed, HF antenna staged for evening stand-up, WINLINK accounts confirmed.
- Verify **Medical Unit readiness** with MAJ Gampe — supplies on hand, casualty point identified.
- Brief the IC at the morning brief on Logistics readiness.

### 13 MAY (evening) — HF Antenna Stand-Up

- Per LTC Estes' 26 APR direction, **3 RGT stands up the HF antenna 13 MAY evening**. Coordinate with CSM Rutherford and SSG Singh.

### 14 MAY

- **Continuous presence at the Logistics position** at the ICP.
- Manage the **all-day HF Net** — antenna live, WINLINK active, A4U Net at 1300-1330 CST per [Annex H](/docs/comms-plan.md).
- Coordinate with **Medical Unit** during inject windows on injury-response readiness.
- Coordinate with **2LT Neisler (Service Branch)** on hot-spot logistics — water resupply, charging strip rotation, vehicle moves.
- Brief the IC and Ops Chief on any logistics shortfall.

### 15 MAY

- Coordinate **demobilization logistics** — comms teardown, equipment turn-in, vehicle dispersal.
- Lead the **Logistics portion of the AAR**.

## Rank vs role

CPT is appropriate rank for a Logistics Section Chief on this TF. **No internal rank-vs-role tension within the role itself.** Two related dynamics:

- **You're dual-hatted as 4 BN CMDR** — primary duty is Logistics Section on this exercise.
- **Your subordinates include CSM Rutherford** (E-9, the most senior NCO rank in the unit) within the Comms Unit. CPT directing CSM is a normal officer-NCO relationship, but the rank distance is real. Direct via the role; rely on CSM Rutherford's expertise in his Unit; don't duplicate or override on technical comms calls.

## Out of scope (do NOT do)

- **Do not act for the IC** without explicit delegation (Deputy IC is LTC Smith).
- **Do not run Operations** — that's LTC Sheaf.
- **Do not run Planning** — that's 1LT Overton.
- **Do not freelance external coordination** — Liaison Officer routes external traffic.
- **Do not override Medical Unit clinical calls** — back MAJ Gampe.
- **Do not duplicate Comms Unit technical decisions** — CSM Rutherford has the technical depth.
