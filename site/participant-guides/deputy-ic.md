## What this role is

The Deputy IC stands in for the IC when the IC is unavailable, briefs out, on the recon, or otherwise pulled away from the ICP. The Deputy:

- Acts with full IC authority **when the IC is absent and a transition is acknowledged** (typically by the Deputy and at least one Section Chief)
- Serves as the IC's **primary on-staff sounding board** — the one person at the IC's level who can argue with the IC's read of the situation
- Owns **continuity** through shift changes and transitions (e.g., morning brief → execution → evening AAR)
- Holds **a backup line of communication** to DIV / LTC Estes if the IC is unreachable

## Rank vs role

LTC is the appropriate rank for Deputy IC; this is a clean officer billet. Two related dynamics worth keeping in mind:

- **You speak with the IC's voice when the IC is absent** — but only on calls inside the IC's already-stated intent. Decisions that materially change scope, accept new risk, or commit to external taskings should hold for the IC's return unless the situation is time-critical.
- **You outrank the Section Chiefs (Sheaf, Overton, Haddix)** but the ICS structure runs through their billets, not your rank. Direct via the IAP and the Section Chiefs; don't bypass them.

## Out of scope (do NOT do)

- **Do not run a Section** — even though you outrank Section Chiefs. The Deputy IC stays at the IC's level.
- **Do not commit the unit to scope changes** without the IC's concurrence (or genuine time-critical pressure).
- **Do not freelance external coordination** — Liaison Officer or PIO routes external traffic.
- **Do not duplicate the Safety Officer** — back the SO; don't override safety calls.
