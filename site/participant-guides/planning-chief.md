# Planning Section Chief

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Role** | Planning Section Chief (per [ICS-203](/docs/ics-203.md) §5) |
| **Assignee** | **1LT Aaron L. Overton III** (3 RGT ASST S3, MRC-1) |
| **Reports to** | Incident Commander — COL Roark |
| **Section** | Planning |
| **Operational period** | 13-15 MAY 26 |

## What this role is

The Planning Section Chief runs the section that produces the **picture** the IC and Ops Chief act on:

- **Owns the Planning Section** — Deputy (2LT Sobel), RUL (1LT Fielitz-Scarbrough), SITL (1SG Snow), Documentation Unit (CPT McCroskey), Tech Specialists (SSG Miles, WO1 Hendon)
- **Produces the IAP** for each operational period — the Section turns inputs into an ICS-202/203/214/etc. set
- **Synchronizes Section Chief inputs** at planning meetings; translates to a coherent plan
- **Owns documentation discipline** — that the unit's records of decisions, observations, and outcomes survive the exercise
- **Acts as battle captain alternate** for the Operations Section if needed (see [Common](/participant-guides/participant-guides/common.md) "Where to Get Help")

## Expectations during TNMAN-26

### 13 MAY

- On-site at AFRC by **0600**.
- **Lead the Planning Section setup** — boards up, tables in place, ICS forms inventory verified, web portal availability confirmed (or hardcopy fallback ready).
- Coordinate with the **Documentation Unit (CPT McCroskey)** on records flow.
- Coordinate with the **SITL (1SG Snow)** on initial picture and CalTopo verification.
- Coordinate with the **RUL (1LT Fielitz-Scarbrough)** on sign-in and personnel data flow.
- Brief the IC at the morning brief on Planning Section readiness.

### 14 MAY

- **Continuous presence at the Planning rail.**
- Run the **planning meeting** (informal — section chiefs gather around the rail) at each inject transition.
- Review the **Section ICS-214 stream** as it lands; flag gaps to Section Chiefs.
- Coordinate **AAR-observation capture** with all Section Chiefs.
- Brief the IC at any change in Planning Section status.

### 15 MAY

- Lead the **Planning portion of the AAR** — picture quality, documentation, planning-meeting cadence.
- **Collate AAR observations** from all sections; deliver to the XO and IC for the DIV submission.

## Rank vs role

This is a substantial rank-vs-role billet. **1LT Overton — a junior officer — directs a section that includes a CPT (McCroskey), a 1SG (Snow), an SSG (Miles), and a WO1 (Hendon).** This is normal in ICS and increasingly normal in TNSG given staffing realities.

- **Within the Planning Section, authority runs through the role.** Section Chief decisions on resource allocation, rotation, AAR submission cadence, and demobilization sequencing carry the weight of the Section regardless of rank distance to subordinates.
- **You direct CPT McCroskey** (Documentation Unit) — a CPT-rank subordinate to a 1LT Section Chief by ICS assignment. McCroskey owns Documentation; you own Section direction. Both work the assignment.
- **You direct 1SG Snow** (Situation Unit Leader) — a 1SG NCO subordinate to a 1LT Section Chief by ICS assignment. The dynamic is also covered in [1SG Snow's role page](/participant-guides/participant-guides/situation-unit-leader.md).
- **You report to COL Roark (IC)** through the standard Section Chief → IC line. No rank-line oddity there.
- **Across the IC, Section Chiefs include LTC Sheaf (Ops) and CPT Haddix (Logistics)** — peer-rank or senior. You're the lowest-ranked Section Chief; the role is what governs in Section-Chief-to-Section-Chief coordination.
- **In NCO-channel matters**, normal rank deference still applies. The 1SG and the CPT carry rank weight outside Section operational direction; you carry it inside.
- **The dynamic is mirrored** by SFC Sturgill as Safety Officer (NCO with stop-work over officers), 1SG Collins as Ops Deputy (NCO Deputy under LTC Section Chief), and SFC Bradley as Logistics Deputy (NCO Deputy under CPT Section Chief). The unit operates this way because the staffing reality requires it; everyone backs everyone in their role.

## Out of scope (do NOT do)

- **Do not run Operations day-to-day** — that's LTC Sheaf. You support; he commands the operational tempo.
- **Do not act for the IC** — that's the Deputy IC.
- **Do not freelance external coordination** — Liaison Officer / IC route external traffic.
- **Do not duplicate the SITL's picture work** — SITL produces; you direct the section.
- **Do not bypass Section Chiefs in coordinating with their sections** — work through them.
