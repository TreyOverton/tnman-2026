## What this role is

The Safety Officer is the IC's risk-reduction authority. In ICS, the SO holds **stop-work authority** that operates regardless of rank. The SO:

- **Identifies hazards** — environmental (heat, weather, road), tactical (fatigue, civilian interaction), procedural (briefing gaps, comms loss)
- **Has stop-work authority** for any activity creating an unacceptable safety risk; the IC backs the call
- **Coordinates with the Medical Unit** (MAJ Gampe) on injury, exposure, hydration response
- **Briefs safety considerations** at the morning brief and at any change in conditions (weather, tempo)
- **Owns the [DD Form 2977](/docs/dd2977.md)** posture for the operational period — overall residual risk is **Moderate**

## Rank vs role

This is the page's central rank-vs-role example. **SFC Sturgill — an NCO — holds stop-work authority over officers up to and including COL Roark.** This is doctrinally normal in ICS and Army Composite Risk Management, and it should be normal in practice on this exercise.

- **The Safety Officer's stop-work call is non-negotiable in the moment.** When SFC Sturgill says "stop," activity stops. Disagreement runs after the fact, through the IC.
- **Across rank lines**, the SO's authority is the role — a SFC may direct a CPT to halt or correct an unsafe action, and the CPT complies. The same expectation applies to the IC.
- **In informal interactions**, normal rank deference still applies. The SO leans on rank only when the role is engaged.
- **The SO defers to the IC** on questions that aren't pure safety — scope, tempo, mission essentiality. The IC may overrule a stop-work call **after** the activity is paused, but only by accepting the documented risk in writing (DD 2977 Block 4 amendment or equivalent).

This dynamic also applies in spirit to the RUL (1LT Fielitz-Scarbrough) directing CPT McCroskey, and the Planning Chief (1LT Overton) directing 1SG Snow — see those pages.

## Out of scope (do NOT do)

- **Do not run Operations** — that's the Operations Section Chief (LTC Sheaf).
- **Do not function as Medical** — coordinate with MAJ Gampe; he's the Medical Unit lead.
- **Do not engage external agencies** on safety questions outside the IC's awareness.
- **Do not waive safety standards** to keep an inject moving — call the stop, then negotiate the path forward with the IC.
