## What this role is

You are the **boots on the ground** for this exercise. The 3-PAX squad is the only physical SECFOR foot patrol on the screen line — two other screens are tabletop / notional. Your role:

- **Physical perimeter presence** parallel to the southernmost side of US-11W (outside the HAAP fence)
- **Observation and reporting** — anything you see at NAIs 3001-3010, civilians, vehicles, activity
- **Comms node** — at least one TACN/VHF radio carried by the squad; ICS-213 message format with `EXERCISE EXERCISE EXERCISE` per [Annex H](../docs/comms-plan.md)
- **CalTopo location dot** — the ICP watches your position via the team map
- **Civilian-interaction restraint** — presence only, no detention, no law-enforcement function

The squad is **2 PAX short of a doctrinal 5-man squad** (PV1 Elrod was removed 30 APR — see [Roster](../roster.md)). Sheaf's framing: *"one almost-actual patrol and two notional on the screen line."*

## Expectations during TNMAN-26

### 13 MAY (light day)

- On-site at AFRC by **0600** if you're on the 13 MAY roster (verify with SSG Whalen and the SECFOR Deputy).
- Receive the **14 MAY foot-patrol brief** from 2LT Garrison and SFC Ferguson — position, NO-GO Line, civilian posture, hydration, comms, CalTopo.
- Verify your **CalTopo dot is live** at sign-in.
- Verify **TACN/VHF radio** assignment and radio check with the Comms Unit.
- Confirm **water and personal supplies** for 14 MAY.

### 14 MAY (main day)

- **0600 sign-in at AFRC**.
- **0900-1000 squad brief** with 2LT Garrison; final radio check.
- **~1000 release** to the foot-patrol position parallel to southernmost US-11W.
- **Maintain position** through the operational day — patrol pattern within the assigned segment, regular comms check-ins, observation logging.
- During the **1400-1430 sUAS event**, **observe and report** any sUAS activity over your position to the SECFOR Branch and Situation Unit. **Do not engage** sUAS — observation only.
- **Hydrate every 30 minutes**; rotate rest among the 3 squad members.
- **Log observations on ICS-214** (hardcopy or via the web portal once live).
- **Return to the ICP** by ~2030 for sign-out, ICS-214 close, AAR capture.

### 15 MAY (light day)

- Sign-in at AFRC; participate in AAR.
- Capture AAR observations specific to the foot-patrol experience.

## Rank vs role

The squad has **no officer in the foot-patrol element itself.** SSG Whalen leads as squad leader (an NCO leading two junior enlisted). 2LT Garrison directs the squad from the ICP via radio.

- **SSG Whalen (squad leader)** — runs the squad on the ground. Tasking, rest cycle, immediate decisions on the position. Reports to 2LT Garrison via radio for tasking changes.
- **PFC Human and PV2 Elrod** — execute the squad leader's direction; observe and report.
- **2LT Garrison directs SSG Whalen by role** — a 2LT giving direction to an SSG by ICS role assignment, even though SSG Whalen is more senior in NCO time. Normal in ICS; both Whalen and Garrison work the assignment.
- **In the field**, SSG Whalen has on-the-ground judgment authority — if conditions require a position change, a stop, or a return to the ICP for a real-world reason, his call holds. He coordinates with Garrison after the action, not before.

## Out of scope (do NOT do)

- **DO NOT** cross the HAAP fence into the perimeter.
- **DO NOT** detain, restrict, or interact with civilians beyond minimum courtesy. **Call 911** for any real-world situation; notify SSG Whalen → 2LT Garrison → SECFOR Branch.
- **DO NOT** capture imagery within the HAAP fence (per LTC Sheaf 11 APR).
- **DO NOT** engage sUAS during the 1400-1430 event — observe and report only.
- **DO NOT** leave the assigned screen segment without SECFOR Branch direction.
- **DO NOT** carry Personally Owned Weapons — explicitly NOT authorized per [RGT Orders 26-04-601-3](../docs/rgt-orders.md).
