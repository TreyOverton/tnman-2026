## What this role is

The Comms Unit runs **all task-force communications** for the operational period:

- **HF Net participation** per [Annex H](/docs/comms-plan.md) — 3 RGT call sign **AAN4ZR** (HHC AAN4ZX, 3 BN AAN4AB, 2 BN AAN4AH)
- **A4U Net check-in** at 1300-1330 CST on 14 MAY and 15 MAY (Net Control: AAN4YR at TNG JOC)
- **WINLINK** account active and password current — secondary to HF voice per PACE
- **VHF channel TNSG04** for Mt. Carmel Site Defense per Annex H
- **TACN radios** — 3 RGT requires ~3 TACN radios; coordinate with LTC Hughes (G6) for issuance
- **ICS-309 station logs** — maintained throughout the operational period
- **ICS-213 message form** — line 7 begins and ends with `EXERCISE EXERCISE EXERCISE`
- Coordination with **CW2 Roach (2 RGT 51st SIG)** as the comms-plan owner across the exercise

## Expectations during TNMAN-26

### 13 MAY

- On-site by **0600**.
- **Set up the Comms position** in the ICP — HF transceiver staged, VHF radios distributed, WINLINK laptop online.
- Verify **WINLINK account** at AAN4ZR — password current, Pactor / VARA / Telnet connection tested.
- Distribute **TACN radios** to SECFOR Branch and Air Ops Branch; radio check.
- **Stand up the HF antenna in the evening** per LTC Estes 26 APR direction. Test propagation.
- Brief CPT Haddix on Comms readiness.

### 14 MAY

- **Continuous Comms position coverage.**
- **A4U Net at 1300-1330 CST** — open net to NCS (AAN4YR), provide radio check, exchange WINLINK SPOTREP-2 / SITREP traffic as requested.
- **Stress HF throughout the day** per LTC Estes 27 APR direction — get repetitions on voice and digital.
- Coordinate with **SECFOR Branch** on foot-patrol radio traffic.
- Coordinate with **Air Ops Branch** on flight-coordination comms.
- **Maintain ICS-309 station log** — every transmission logged.
- Brief CPT Haddix on Comms status; brief the IC on any HF/TACN issue.

### 15 MAY

- Continue HF Net participation through the half-day.
- **Tear down comms equipment** at end of day; coordinate with Logistics Deputy.
- Hand the **ICS-309 station logs** to the Documentation Unit.
- Capture Comms-specific AAR observations — HF propagation, TACN performance, WINLINK reliability.

## Rank vs role

Notable rank dynamic. **CSM Rutherford is E-9, the most senior NCO rank in the unit.** He is a Unit Leader within the Logistics Section, reporting to **CPT Haddix (O-3)**. SSG Singh (E-6) supports as the second comms operator.

- **CSM-as-Unit-Leader is unusual but appropriate here.** CSM Rutherford has the technical depth (3 RGT S6 NCO primary duty). The Comms Unit is technically demanding, and Rutherford runs it well. CPT Haddix directs at the Section level; Rutherford runs the Unit.
- **Rank distance from Section Chief is real.** CPT Haddix should direct via the role and rely on CSM Rutherford's technical authority within the Unit. Don't duplicate or override on technical comms calls. Trust the CSM.
- **In NCO-channel matters**, CSM Rutherford carries weight throughout the unit including with junior officers. The Section-Chief-to-Unit-Leader dynamic is one channel; the senior-NCO dynamic is another.
- **SSG Singh's primary duty is 3 BN BN S6** — the dual-hat aligns naturally with the Comms Unit role.
- **MRC-2 Rutherford** is ICP-interior per DD 2977. SSG Singh (MRC-1) can deploy to the screen position if a forward radio operator is needed (TNSG04 channel).

## Out of scope (do NOT do)

- **Do not engage external HF stations** outside the Annex H plan without coordination with **CW2 Roach (2 RGT — comms-plan owner)** and G6.
- **Do not modify frequencies** without NCS direction (announced via voice and WINLINK).
- **Do not run radio traffic for SECFOR or Air Ops** without their callsigns — those Branches own their channel traffic.
- **Do not duplicate Documentation Unit work** — you produce ICS-309; they archive it.
- **Do not freelance external comms with HAAP, TEMA, TDOT** — route through the IC / Liaison.
