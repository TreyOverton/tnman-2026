## What this role is

The Regimental CSM is the senior enlisted advisor to the IC and the senior enlisted authority on the task force. The role is **advisory and influential, not command-line.** The RCSM:

- Counsels the IC on **enlisted morale, discipline, standards, and soldier care**
- Walks the floor — **eyes on the team** the way the IC and Section Chiefs cannot from the command rail
- Mentors NCOs across sections (1SG Snow, 1SG Collins, SFC Sturgill, SFC Bradley, SFC Ferguson, etc.)
- Champions soldier issues that don't have a natural section path (housing, equipment, fatigue, morale)
- Represents the unit at any external NCO-channel coordination

The RCSM is **not** in the IAP-execution chain. Section Chiefs run their sections; the RCSM runs influence.

## Expectations during TNMAN-26

### 13 MAY

- Visible presence at sign-in; greet personnel as they arrive.
- Walk through staging, ICP buildout, and the screen-patrol position; check that NCOs have what they need.
- Brief the IC on any morale / standards / discipline issues observed during the day.

### 14 MAY

- Continue floor presence — rotation between ICP, staging, screen patrol, Air Ops.
- During inject windows, stay close to the IC for senior-NCO counsel if needed.
- Track team energy and fatigue across the day; flag heat / hydration / morale issues to the **Safety Officer** and **Chaplain** before they become incidents.

### 15 MAY

- Lead the NCO portion of the internal AAR — what the team observed about leadership, standards, training value.
- Sign off NCO-channel close-out.

## Rank vs role

The RCSM is the senior NCO on the task force. The role is a Command Staff advisory billet — **the RCSM operates by influence and counsel, not by giving direction within the IAP.**

- **Within the IAP execution chain**, the RCSM does not direct sections. Section Chiefs run their sections; tasking comes through them.
- **Across rank channels**, the RCSM has standing to engage any NCO on the task force on standards, discipline, mentorship, or morale — independent of section assignment.
- **The RCSM speaks for the IC's expectations of the enlisted force** without speaking for the IC on operational decisions. The line is real; the RCSM holds it.

## Out of scope (do NOT do)

- **Do not direct Section operations.** That's the Section Chief's lane.
- **Do not override Safety Officer calls.** Back the SO.
- **Do not step into S1 / Resources Unit work** — personnel records run through the RUL (1LT Fielitz-Scarbrough).
- **Do not engage external commands** on TF business without IC concurrence — the Liaison Officer routes external traffic.
