## What this role is

The Situation Unit Leader (SITL) owns the **operational picture** for the task force — the consolidated view of what is happening, what has just happened, and what may be about to happen. The SITL:

- Maintains the **common operational picture (COP)** displayed in the ICP — boards, maps, NAI status, threat picture
- Produces and distributes **SITREPs** at regular intervals and on significant events
- Tracks the **NAI overlay** (3001 ICP through 3010 US-11W per [Ops Graphics](/docs/ops-graphics.md)) and any reported activity at each NAI
- Integrates **observations from SECFOR, Air Ops, Comms, and external partners** into the picture
- Hands the IC and Operations Section Chief a **timely, accurate read** that supports their decisions
- Watches the **CalTopo team map** alongside the Safety Officer for personnel disposition

This is doctrinally an **S2 (Intelligence) function** at unit echelon. In ICS, it is the Planning Section's window into "what is the situation right now." It is **not** the same as MSEL / inject control (that's exercise control / DIV) and **not** the same as the AAR record (Documentation Unit owns that).

## Expectations during TNMAN-26

### 13 MAY (ICP IOC)

- Stand up the **SITAware boards** in the ICP (NAI list, current weather, friendly disposition, comms status, MEDEVAC routes).
- Brief the **AO and NAIs** at the morning brief.
- With the **RUL (1LT Fielitz-Scarbrough)**, verify each participant's **CalTopo dot is live** at sign-in.
- Capture the **initial picture** by 1200; first SITREP out by 1300.
- Confirm the **HF Net check-in cadence** with the Comms Unit — 1300-1330 CST per [Annex H](/docs/comms-plan.md).

### 14 MAY (Main Day)

- **Rolling SITREP every 2-3 hours**, or upon significant event.
- **Inject support:** when an inject lands (1200 waterway, 1300 OPFOR, 1400-1430 UAS), capture the activity, update the NAI status board, and feed the Operations Section.
- Track **SECFOR observations** — anything the screen patrol or Air Ops sees that touches an NAI.
- Feed **PERSTAT updates to Chief Hogan** (HHC LNO at JOC) per Estes 26 APR direction.
- Maintain the **CalTopo team map** as the day's location-of-record for personnel.
- **Brief the IC** at any change of operational tempo.

### 15 MAY (Half-Day + AAR)

- **Final SITREP** (close-of-exercise) summarizing 14 MAY activity.
- Capture **AAR observations** specific to intelligence / SA gaps.
- Hand off the SITAware boards to the **Documentation Unit (CPT McCroskey)** for archive.

## Rank vs role

This role is doctrinally an **S2 officer** position — typically a 2LT through MAJ in a fully-staffed unit. **3 RGT does not have an S2 officer assigned**; 1SG Snow is the S2 NCO and is filling the unit-leader role directly.

This is normal in TNSG and reflects the broader Tennessee State Guard reality: state-wide TNSG is at roughly **4% of nominal full structure**. NCOs are routinely filling officer billets, and lower-ranking officers are routinely filling roles that would be held by senior officers in a federal-component unit. **It works, but it requires explicit awareness from everyone in the role and around it.**

For 1SG Snow as SITL, specifically:

- **Authority within the Situation Unit** runs through the **role**, not the rank. 1SG Snow directs the unit's work, briefs the IC and Ops Chief, and signs the SITREPs.
- **Officers without an S2 background** seeking situational awareness — Logistics, Air Ops, etc. — **defer to 1SG Snow on the picture itself.** He has the operational read.
- **1SG Snow defers to 1LT Overton** on Section-Chief-level decisions (resource allocation, rotation, demobilization sequencing). That's how the ICS structure is wired — and 1LT Overton is junior in rank but the right billet.
- **1SG Snow defers to the IC and Ops Chief** on operational decisions that follow from the picture (e.g., where to move a screen patrol). His job is to give them an accurate read; their job is to decide.
- **Outside the Section, 1SG Snow's authority runs through his role title (SITL),** not his rank. When he says *"the Situation Unit needs X,"* that's an SITL direction, and the response should be the same regardless of whether the other party is a 2LT, a 1LT, or another 1SG.
- **In informal interactions, his rank still matters.** He's a senior NCO and carries the authority of the rank in personal/professional matters and in NCO-channel mentoring. Operational direction within the ICP runs through ICS structure; NCO-channel direction runs through rank.

The same dynamic applies in reverse to **1LT Overton (Planning Section Chief)** — a 1LT giving direction to a 1SG (Snow), a CPT (McCroskey), and an SSG/WO1 (Miles, Hendon). Section-Chief authority flows through the billet, not the rank insignia. Everyone on the team is asked to act on this consistently.

## Out of scope (do NOT do)

- **Do not direct SECFOR positioning.** That's the **SECFOR Branch Director (MAJ Crosby)** under the Operations Section Chief. The SITL informs; SECFOR moves.
- **Do not brief externally** (HAAP POC, DIV CG, media). That's the **IC** and the **Liaison Officer / PIO**. The SITL feeds those briefings; doesn't deliver them.
- **Do not own personnel rosters.** That's the **Resources Unit (RUL: 1LT Fielitz-Scarbrough)**. The SITL uses RUL data to keep the picture current; doesn't maintain the source.
- **Do not own the AAR record.** That's the **Documentation Unit (CPT McCroskey)**. The SITL captures observations and submits them; the Documentation Unit archives and routes.
- **Do not run MSEL or schedule injects.** That's exercise control / DIV. The SITL responds to injects; doesn't drive them.
- **Do not carry imagery off-station.** Per [LTC Sheaf 11 APR](/comms.md) — no imagery within the HAAP fence; what is captured stays controlled within the ICP per the [JAG UAS Memo](/docs/jag-uas-memo.md) guidance.
- **Do not operate sUAS or radios as a primary.** **Air Ops** and the **Comms Unit** are separate teams. The SITL is a consumer of their products.
