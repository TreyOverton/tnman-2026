## What this role is

The JAG / Legal Officer is the IC's special staff advisor on legal questions arising during the operational period. In ICS, legal advisors sit in Command Staff alongside Safety, PIO, and Liaison Officer; in Army doctrine, the Staff Judge Advocate is a special staff officer with direct access to the commander. On TNMAN-26, this role:

- **Advises the IC** on questions of authority (SAD vs Title 10), Rules of Engagement, civilian interaction, drone privacy law (TCA § 39-13-609), and other legal exposure that surfaces during execution.
- **Is the secondary legal channel** for the exercise. The primary legal review channel — for any consultation that needs a written response or a formal opinion — runs through **COL Williams (DIV PMO)** per [DIV OPORD §3.i.6.(h)](/docs/opord-div.md). CPT McCroskey is the on-call surface for fast in-the-moment questions; anything formal routes through Williams.
- **Does not deploy physically** for TNMAN-26. He is reachable by phone or email during the operational windows; no physical billet at the ICP.

## Expectations during TNMAN-26

### Pre-exercise (NLT 12 MAY)

- Confirm phone availability with the IC and the ICP duty officer (1LT Overton). Establish the call-back expectation (e.g., "answer or call back within 30 min during 0600-2030 on 13-14 MAY").
- Review the three submitted JAG pre-briefs to be familiar with the questions already in flight:
  - [Command Authority & Mission Sets](/docs/jag-brief.md)
  - [Personally-Owned DJI UAS](/docs/jag-brief-dji.md)

- Read the **[Risk Assessment](/risk.md) Hazards 7 (ROE), 8 (Title 10 / SAD seam), and 9 (TCA § 39-13-609)** so the most likely real-time legal questions during execution are pre-loaded.

### 13 MAY (operational period 1)

- **Phone available 0600-2030.**
- Likely call topics — **ROE clarification** at the perimeter, **civilian interaction** at TCPs, **UAS / TCA questions** if pilots fly outside the planned envelope, **HAAP-perimeter authority** questions if a real-event handoff occurs.
- Standing posture for routine questions: **brief the IC, decline to opine on anything that warrants a formal review, route those to COL Williams.** Speed matters more than polish; the IC needs a working answer to keep tempo.

### 14 MAY (operational period 2 — main exercise day)

- **Phone available 0600-2030.** Highest-volume day — 3 BN main-gate security test (141300R), Phase II ENDEX (NLT 142131R), inject scenarios (Soldier KIA / Downed UAS / TBD) all may surface JAG questions.
- Coordinate with the **Documentation Unit** (1LT Overton, dual-hat) on call records — every consultation logged via ICS-214 entry on the ICP side.

### 15 MAY (AAR day)

- Not on call. AAR participation by the JAG/Legal Officer is **post-exercise** if requested, asynchronously.

## Rank vs role

Not a rank-vs-role billet on this exercise — CPT McCroskey is the natural rank for an ASST JAG on the IC's special staff. Worth noting:

- **Special-staff access trumps section-channel routing.** Legal questions go straight to the JAG / Legal Officer (and the IC), bypassing the section structure. The JAG advisor speaks in the IC's name on legal matters.
- **The advisory voice is not a command voice.** CPT McCroskey advises; the IC decides. If a stop-action call is required for a legal reason, the IC (or the Safety Officer, on safety grounds) makes it — not the JAG / Legal Officer.

## Out of scope (do NOT do)

- **Do not issue formal legal opinions in real time.** Anything that needs a written answer routes to COL Williams (DIV PMO).
- **Do not act as a section advisor** within Operations, Planning, or Logistics — Command Staff sits laterally to the sections.
- **Do not engage external agencies** on legal questions outside the IC's awareness — every external legal contact on this exercise routes through the IC and DIV PMO.
- **Do not assume primary documentation duty** — that role moved to 1LT Overton under the 4 MAY reassignment (see [Documentation Unit guide](/participant-guides/participant-guides/documentation-unit.md)).
