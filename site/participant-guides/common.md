## Activity Logging — ICS-214

Every participant produces an **ICS-214 Activity Log** for each operational period (13, 14, 15 MAY). Capture:

- Major task starts and completions
- Personnel encountered or coordinated with
- Equipment issued, used, or returned
- Any incident — safety, security, comms, civilian interaction
- Observations that may belong in the AAR

**Submission:** A web portal for live ICS-214 entry is **in development** by 1LT Overton. The portal will auto-capture identity (auth), timestamp, and (where possible) GPS coordinate when an entry is submitted. **Until the portal is live**, an ICS-214 hardcopy or shared spreadsheet template is acceptable. Submit your completed 214 to the **Documentation Unit (CPT McCroskey)** at the end of each operational period.

## After-Action Observations

Capture **AAR-relevant observations as they happen** — don't wait until 15 MAY to remember.

What counts:

- Things that worked well (keep doing)
- Things that did not work (stop doing)
- Things to start doing differently
- Honest comments on training value, equipment, comms, schedule, briefing quality

The same web portal will accept AAR notes. **Until it's live**, capture in your ICS-214 (mark `AAR:` prefix) or a personal notebook. Submit observations to **1LT Overton (Planning Section Chief)** as well as your Section Chief. The XO will collate the post-exercise AAR.

## CalTopo — Location Sharing

The ICP needs to be able to see where personnel are during the exercise window. Every participant runs **CalTopo** on a personal mobile device. This is non-negotiable for accountability and for the **Safety Officer (SFC Sturgill)**.

See **[CalTopo Guide](participant-guides/caltopo.md)** for full setup. Critical points:

- Install before 12 MAY
- Allow location **"Always"** (not "While Using App") — iOS in particular requires this
- Join the shared TNMAN-26 team map (link shared in the participant briefing)
- Verify your check-in 30 minutes before report time on 13 MAY

Privacy: location is visible only to the ICP team via the shared map; it is not retained beyond the exercise.

## ROE Summary

TNSG is **unarmed and presence-only** on this exercise. We are **perimeter-only outside the HAAP fence line**. We do not enter the perimeter fence. We do not detain civilians. We do not act as law enforcement.

If a real-world situation develops, **call 911**, then notify the **IC (COL Roark)** and **Safety Officer (SFC Sturgill)** immediately.

## Imagery / sUAS Constraints

Per **LTC Sheaf 11 APR** and **Mr. Armstrong (HAAP POC) 20 APR**:

- **Do not capture imagery or video within the HAAP perimeter fence line.** This includes personal phone photos.

## Personal Mobile Phones

Encouraged for emergency comms, CalTopo location sharing, and quick coordination. **Not** for:
