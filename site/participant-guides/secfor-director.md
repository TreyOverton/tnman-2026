## What this role is

The SECFOR Branch Director commands the security-force element on the exercise — the team that physically secures the perimeter (within the constraints HAAP has set) and notionally exercises the broader screen / patrol / QRF posture.

- **Commands the SECFOR Branch** — Deputy (SFC Ferguson) and the Screen Division/Group Leader (2LT Garrison)
- Decides **patrol positioning, rotation, and tempo** for the actual physical foot patrol on US-11W (and the two notional patrols)
- **Coordinates with HAAP POC** (Mr. Armstrong) through the IC / Liaison on any perimeter-adjacent activity
- Receives **inject response taskings** from the Ops Chief on the OPFOR main-gate scenario (1300) and the sUAS detection event (1400-1430)
- Owns **SECFOR safety** as it interacts with civilian US-11W traffic, weather, and fatigue

The SECFOR mission is **perimeter-only, unarmed, presence-only** — see [ROE summary in Common Expectations](/participant-guides/participant-guides/common.md).

## Rank vs role

MAJ is appropriate for a Branch Director on this TF. **No internal rank-vs-role tension within the SECFOR Branch.** Two related dynamics:

- **You are also the 3 BN CO** — a dual-hat. On this exercise you operate as SECFOR Branch Director under the Ops Section Chief; your 3 BN CO authority is contextually backgrounded. If something exposed a 3 BN-internal personnel issue (e.g., one of your A Co MPs needing immediate counsel), you may briefly engage as 3 BN CO and then return to the Branch.
- **The other Branch Director is CPT Borrilez (Air Ops)** — peer-rank but you both report to LTC Sheaf as Ops Section Chief. Coordinate horizontally; route through Sheaf for any cross-Branch dispute.

## Out of scope (do NOT do)

- **Do not direct Air Ops** — that's CPT Borrilez.
- **Do not commit SECFOR PAX inside the HAAP fence** — perimeter only.
- **Do not act as Safety Officer** — coordinate with SFC Sturgill, but stop-work calls are his.
- **Do not engage media or external civilians** beyond minimum courtesy — route to PIO / IC / Liaison.
- **Do not skip the Mr. Armstrong NO-GO line** — even for a perceived training opportunity.
