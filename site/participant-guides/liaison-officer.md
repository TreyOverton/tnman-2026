## What this role is

The Liaison Officer is the **coordination point** between the 3 RGT TF and external organizations:

- **Incoming LNOs** from 2 RGT, 4 RGT, and DIV (4 PAX total per CG intent)
- **HAAP** — Mr. Tony Armstrong and his team
- **TNARNG / JFHQ-TN** — through the JOC LNO chain (Hughes / Barbas / Coulter / Jernigan / Hogan)
- **TEMA, TDOT, local LE** — if engagement is required during the exercise window
- **Visiting officials** — DV traffic, BG Cox if visiting, etc.

The TF Liaison's responsibility is to make sure the right external person gets routed to the right internal person, fast. The Liaison does **not** speak for the unit — they connect, coordinate, and clarify.

The **incoming LNOs** are integrated team members under the IAP, working alongside the TF Liaison. They bring **two-way visibility** — their parent commands see what 3 RGT is doing, and 3 RGT sees what their parent commands need.

## Rank vs role

A few related dynamics:

- **Incoming LNOs may be peer or senior in rank** to the TF Liaison Officer. The TF LO integrates them under the IAP; rank is irrelevant within the IAP-execution chain.
- **Incoming LNOs report through their parent commands**, not through the 3 RGT TF — so the Liaison Officer's coordination authority over them is **functional, not command**. Treat them as integrated team members, not subordinates.
- **The 4 RGT LNOs** (potentially MAJ Thomas / MAJ Hamlin per 28 APR comms meeting — unverified) may outrank the TF Liaison Officer. Same dynamic.
- **The Liaison does not speak for the IC** unless explicitly delegated. External requests that exceed routine coordination go to the IC.

## Out of scope (do NOT do)

- **Do not commit the TF** to scope changes from external requests without IC concurrence.
- **Do not engage media** — that's the PIO.
- **Do not direct sections** — coordinate; route; don't task.
- **Do not duplicate the JOC LNOs** — Hughes / Barbas / Coulter / Jernigan / Hogan run the JOC integration. The TF Liaison routes through them, not around them.
