# Staging Area Manager

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Role** | Staging Area Manager (per [ICS-203](/docs/ics-203.md) §7) |
| **Assignee** | **MAJ Curtis R. Rookard** (HHC Eng Officer, MRC-1) |
| **Reports to** | Operations Section Chief — LTC Sheaf |
| **Section** | Operations |
| **Operational period** | 13-15 MAY 26 |

## What this role is

The Staging Area Manager owns **personnel and resource flow into and out of the ICP**:

- **Sign-in / sign-out** of all participants each day (with the **RUL** for personnel records)
- **Vehicle staging** — POVs, the TACN-radio kit, charging strip, water/snacks, any equipment kits
- **Ready-line management** — when SECFOR releases for the screen patrol, when Air Ops launches, when Medical deploys
- **Demobilization sequencing** — who leaves when, in what order, with what accountability check
- Coordination with the **Logistics Section** on physical setup and teardown of the staging area

The Staging Area is the **first and last face of the ICP** — first stop in the morning, last stop at night.

## Expectations during TNMAN-26

### 13 MAY

- **On-site by 0530** to set up the staging area before 0600 sign-in.
- Coordinate with the **Logistics Deputy (SFC Bradley)** on physical setup — sign-in tables, vehicle parking, charging strip, water/snacks.
- Run **0600 sign-in**: every participant signs in, has their CalTopo dot verified (with the **Situation Unit**), and is briefed on the day's flow.
- During ICP IOC sequence, manage **release** of SECFOR to the screen position, Air Ops to the launch site, Medical to the casualty point.
- Brief the **Ops Section Chief** on staging readiness.

### 14 MAY

- **Run sign-in** at 0600.
- Manage **inject support** — when an inject lands, who needs to move, what equipment goes with them.
- Track **vehicle disposition** — which POVs are at the ICP, which are at the screen position, which have departed.
- Coordinate with the **Comms Unit** if a participant needs a radio swap mid-day.
- **Sign-out** at end of day; verify CalTopo deactivation if appropriate.

### 15 MAY

- Run sign-in for the half-day.
- Manage **demobilization sequencing** — who leaves first (long-distance commuters, MRC-3 single-day Soldiers if any are on a 15 MAY rotation, etc.), who leaves last.
- Verify final **personnel accountability** with the RUL.

## Rank vs role

MAJ is high rank for a Staging Area Manager — typical ICS doctrine has this role at SFC-CPT level on a TF this size. The assignment reflects that **MAJ Rookard is the right person for the responsibility** (Engineer Officer with experience managing flow and accountability), not that the role normally calls for his rank.

- **Subordinates in this role include SFCs and 1SGs** (e.g., SFC Bradley as Logistics Deputy may overlap with you on physical setup). **Coordination, not command** — Bradley reports to CPT Haddix in Logistics.
- **You direct participants of all ranks** through sign-in and staging — the role's authority is the role, not the rank. A SGT, a CPT, and a CSM all sign in with you and follow your release direction.

## Out of scope (do NOT do)

- **Do not run Logistics setup details** — that's CPT Haddix and SFC Bradley.
- **Do not direct SECFOR or Air Ops once they release** — that's the Branch Directors.
- **Do not maintain the personnel roster** — that's the RUL (1LT Fielitz-Scarbrough). You consume their data.
- **Do not freelance the demobilization order** — coordinate with the Ops Chief and IC on departure timing.
