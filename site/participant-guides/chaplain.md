## What this role is

The Chaplain is the unit's spiritual and personal-welfare advisor. The role:

- **Provides ministry presence** during the exercise — visible, available, low-pressure
- **Holds privileged communication** with personnel (subject to chaplain-corps doctrine)
- Counsels the IC and Command Staff on personnel welfare matters
- Is available for **family-emergency notification** (in coordination with S1) and personal crisis response
- Offers **invocations / inspirational moments** at IC's discretion (morning brief, AAR, etc.)

The Chaplain operates outside the operational reporting chain. Conversations with the Chaplain are confidential by default.

## Expectations during TNMAN-26

### 13 MAY

- Be present at the morning brief and ICP IOC sequence; visible to the team without being underfoot.
- Walk staging, screen patrol, and ICP; light personal interaction with personnel.
- If asked, offer a brief invocation at sign-in or morning brief — IC's call.

### 14 MAY

- **Continued presence** throughout the operational day.
- Be reachable during inject stress moments; the team will look for steadying influences.
- Coordinate quietly with the **RCSM** and **Safety Officer** on any personnel showing signs of acute stress or emergency.

### 15 MAY

- Offer a closing reflection at the AAR / debrief if invited by the IC.

## Rank vs role

MAJ is appropriate rank for the Chaplain billet. The Chaplain operates under **chaplain-corps doctrine**, which sits parallel to standard rank dynamics:

- **Chaplain authority comes from the role**, not the rank. Personnel of any rank may approach the Chaplain in confidence regardless of rank distance.
- **Privileged communication** is honored across the chain — the Chaplain does not relay confidential conversations to the IC, RCSM, or Section Chiefs without consent (or the narrow exceptions chaplain-corps doctrine carves out for safety / mandatory-report situations).
- The Chaplain's MRC-4 status is being verified separately (see [Roster](../roster.md) Open Items).

## Out of scope (do NOT do)

- **Do not function as a tactical advisor** — IC, Ops Chief, and Section Chiefs run operations.
- **Do not function as a Safety Officer** — that's SFC Sturgill.
- **Do not maintain personnel records** — that's the RUL (1LT Fielitz-Scarbrough).
- **Do not engage external organizations** on behalf of the unit without IC direction.
