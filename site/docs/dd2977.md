## Standing Mission Constraints (from CG and HAAP)

- **Dawn-to-dusk duty day** — Per CG direction, TNSG participation in TNMAN 26 is limited to daylight hours. No overnight assembly at the ICP.
- **RON at home or hotel** — Soldiers participating on more than one day must return home (or to a hotel of their choosing) overnight between duty days. *Narrow carve-out:* [JFHQ-TN FRAGORD 3](/docs/fragord-3-jfhq.md) tasks 278th ACR to provide **50-PAX RON space at Mt Carmel Armory 11-17 MAY** — this covers **1 RGT OPFOR and TNARNG Security / COMMS personnel**, not a TNSG-wide RON authorization. CG dawn-to-dusk policy still governs TNSG personnel unless separately extended by the CG.
- **Perimeter-only operations at HAAP** — No TNSG personnel are authorized to enter the HAAP premises. All 3 RGT operations occur on the outside perimeter only. The ICP is sited outside the HAAP perimeter. This excludes the interior explosive and industrial hazard set from the risk picture below.
- **Physical SECFOR footprint (HAAP POC, 20 APR — quantified 23 APR)** — SECFOR physical presence is limited to **outside the perimeter fence, parallel to the southernmost side of US 11W** only. Interior N/S/W patrols and screens are **notional (tabletop) only**. The 23 APR TPFDD quantifies the split: **54 Actual PAX on-ground at 14 MAY peak** vs. **70 Notional (tabletop) PAX**; combined Total 124 is retained for sizing/RFF purposes. Medical / hydration / bathroom / parking / chow / transport planning sizes to 54, not 124.
- **Unarmed force** — TNSG is unarmed by default; no Soldiers carry weapons for this mission.
- These constraints apply to all ranks and are baseline controls on every hazard.

### Heat Risk Supplement (Hazard #4 — published per BG Cox 1 MAY 26 review)

**Forecast (published H-13; refresh H-72 and daily).** Kingsport TN / HAAP region for 14 MAY 26 (highest-activity day). Based on Wx Channel / NWS climatology and current outlook: high temp **78–82 °F**, dewpoint 60–65 °F, RH 55–70%. Projected **WBGT 78–83 °F → Heat Category 1–2 (White / Green flag)**. NWS hourly forecast for Kingsport (ZIP 37660) re-pulled at H-72 and posted at the ICP daily; 14 MAY forecast re-evaluated each morning of execution.

**Day-of measurement.** Kestrel 5400 (or equivalent WBGT meter) operated by the Safety Officer; if no meter is available on-site, NWS hourly Heat Index for Kingsport (ZIP 37660) substitutes. Readings logged at **0700, 0900, 1100, 1300, 1500, 1700R**. Safety Officer posts current Heat Category and current work/rest cycle on the ICP whiteboard each hour.

**Work / Rest Cycle and Water Intake (TB MED 507 Table 3-2; FM 7-22 App C):**

| WBGT (°F) | Heat Cat | Flag | Easy Work | Moderate Work | Hard Work | Water (qt/hr) |
|-----------|:--------:|:----:|:---------:|:-------------:|:---------:|:-------------:|
| 78.0 – 81.9 | 1 | White | NL / NL | NL / NL | 40 / 20 min | ½ – ¾ |
| 82.0 – 84.9 | 2 | Green | NL | 50 / 10 min | 30 / 30 min | ¾ – 1 |
| 85.0 – 87.9 | 3 | Yellow | NL | 40 / 20 min | 30 / 30 min | ¾ – 1 |
| 88.0 – 89.9 | 4 | Red | NL | 30 / 30 min | 20 / 40 min | ¾ – 1 |
| ≥ 90.0 | 5 | Black | 50 / 10 min | 20 / 40 min | 10 / 50 min | 1 (max) |

NL = no work-rest limit at that activity level. **Water cap: 1¼ qt/hr (hard work) or 1½ qt/hr (moderate) — never exceed**, to prevent hyponatremia.

**Activity classifications for TNMAN 26.**
- *Easy work:* ICP-interior staff tasks, seated radio operations, command-post duties, sUAS pilot operating from ICP launch station.
- *Moderate work:* Standing TCP, walking patrol on flat paved / improved ground, dismounted screen, perimeter visual reconnaissance.
- *Hard work:* Sustained dismounted patrol off-road or under load. *Not anticipated for this exercise* given perimeter-only and unarmed posture.

**Action thresholds.**
- **Heat Cat 3 (Yellow, 85–87.9 °F WBGT):** S3 + Safety Officer notified; rotate to 40/20 min for moderate outdoor work; reinforce hydration logging; pre-position medical at ICP.
- **Heat Cat 4 (Red, 88–89.9 °F WBGT):** Suspend strenuous outdoor activity; rotate SECFOR to ICP-interior shifts where feasible; medical standby alerted; brief CDR.
- **Heat Cat 5 (Black, ≥ 90 °F WBGT):** Stop all outdoor activity except emergency response; pull SECFOR off perimeter to ICP; brief CDR for go/no-go on continued execution.

**Implementer:** Safety Officer (primary). 3 BN CDR (SECFOR TF CDR) executes the rotation. NCOICs maintain hydration logs and call-overs. CDR holds suspend / no-go authority.
