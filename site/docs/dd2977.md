## Block 1 — Mission / Task Description

**Mission:** 3 RGT, TNSG participates in the Tennessee Maneuver Exercise 2026 (TNMAN-26) — Simulated Kinetic Attack on Holston Army Ammunition Plant (HAAP) — conducting SECFOR operations, small Unmanned Aerial System (sUAS) reconnaissance, Incident Command Post (ICP) support, and comms support across multiple locations in East TN, 13-15 MAY 26, IOT increase MET proficiency and sustain collective and staff training proficiency at the Regimental level.

**References:** [RGT OPORD 26-05-01 (Final, 141600RAPR26)](opord-rgt.md) · [FRAGO 26-05-01.1 (212130RAPR26)](frago-26-05-01-1.md) · [DIV OPORD 26-05 (161200RAPRIL2026)](opord-div.md) · [JFHQ-TN FRAGORD 3](fragord-3-jfhq.md) · [RGT WARNO 26-05-01](warno.md) · [CG's Intent](cg-intent.md) · [Encl A Storyboard](opord-encl-a.md)

## Standing Mission Constraints (from CG and HAAP)

- **Dawn-to-dusk duty day** — Per CG direction, TNSG participation in TNMAN 26 is limited to daylight hours. No overnight assembly at the ICP.
- **RON at home or hotel** — Soldiers participating on more than one day must return home (or to a hotel of their choosing) overnight between duty days. *Narrow carve-out:* [JFHQ-TN FRAGORD 3](fragord-3-jfhq.md) tasks 278th ACR to provide **50-PAX RON space at Mt Carmel Armory 11-17 MAY** — this covers **1 RGT OPFOR and TNARNG Security / COMMS personnel**, not a TNSG-wide RON authorization. CG dawn-to-dusk policy still governs TNSG personnel unless separately extended by the CG.
- **Perimeter-only operations at HAAP** — No TNSG personnel are authorized to enter the HAAP premises. All 3 RGT operations occur on the outside perimeter only. The ICP is sited outside the HAAP perimeter. This excludes the interior explosive and industrial hazard set from the risk picture below.
- **Physical SECFOR footprint (HAAP POC, 20 APR — quantified 23 APR)** — SECFOR physical presence is limited to **outside the perimeter fence, parallel to the southernmost side of US 11W** only. Interior N/S/W patrols and screens are **notional (tabletop) only**. The 23 APR TPFDD quantifies the split: **54 Actual PAX on-ground at 14 MAY peak** vs. **70 Notional (tabletop) PAX**; combined Total 124 is retained for sizing/RFF purposes. Medical / hydration / bathroom / parking / chow / transport planning sizes to 54, not 124.
- **Unarmed force** — TNSG is unarmed by default; no Soldiers carry weapons for this mission.
- These constraints apply to all ranks and are baseline controls on every hazard.

## Block 5 — Overall Residual Risk

**Overall Residual Risk:** <span class="mrc mrc-2">MODERATE</span>

**Rationale:** With all controls applied, every operational hazard line now sits at Low residual:

1. **HAAP interior hazards (O-2)** — excluded by the perimeter-only + ICP-outside-perimeter constraint.
2. **Severe weather (O-1)** — 3 RGT is authorized to call off participation entirely if forecast conditions warrant; "don't go" is a valid control for a training exercise with no real-world response requirement.
3. **Convoy ops (O-3)** — dawn-to-dusk daylight driving only; no vehicles enter HAAP premises; 2-driver rule, 12-hr combined duty/drive cap, authorized self-funded hotels for long commutes.
4. **SECFOR outdoor duty (O-4)** — **TNSG is unarmed by default and no Soldiers will carry weapons**, removing weapon-handling accidents and use-of-force concerns.
5. **Comms blackout inject (O-6)** — PACE plan + personal mobile devices as an independent real-world fallback; the blackout is a training inject, not a real outage.
6. **Medical readiness** — **binary controls** (see Block 4 addendum). Either deploy with a fully mitigated deviation (single-day 14 MAY, ICP-interior role, MRC-1 battle buddy, medical check-ins, dawn-to-dusk, RON at home) or do not deploy at all. Both branches contribute minimal residual exposure.

**Remaining Moderate residuals that drive the aggregate rating:**

- **O-7 Civilian interaction / ROE clarity** at perimeter TCPs (pending JAG-written ROE)
- **L-1 Title 10 / SAD command authority seam** (pending JAG consultation and documented command relationships)

**Reassessment triggers** — any of the following would force a re-rating before execution:

- Weather forecasts at H-72 push O-1 residual above Moderate
- JAG consultation does not conclude before execution (L-1 would climb)
- A mitigation in place on paper cannot be enforced in practice (e.g., inability to provide a dedicated MRC-1 battle buddy for each MRC-3 Soldier, or an MRC-3 Soldier who cannot be held to a single-day schedule)
- Status of an MRC-4 Soldier changes between form signature and execution
