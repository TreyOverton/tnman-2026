## Exercise Overview

Tennessee Maneuvers (TNMAN) and Tennessee Catastrophic Exercise (TNCAT) are full-scale domestic response exercises planned by TNARNG and TEMA. They provide an opportunity for local, state, and federal agencies, National Guard units, and private-sector partners to improve emergency response plans, inter-agency relationships, and interoperability.

**TNMAN / TNCAT 26** is an operations-based functional exercise featuring limited field play, distributed EOC activations, and remote coordination. It tests and improves operations during simultaneous natural hazards and complex threat scenarios, addressing multi-day flooding across Northeast Tennessee combined with cyber and kinetic attacks on critical facilities.

## TNMAN Exercise Objectives

**EO 1 — Conduct Homeland Defense operations in response to ongoing attacks on the homeland.**

- TO 1 — Execute prevention measures (operational coordination, information/intelligence sharing)
- TO 2 — Exercise cybersecurity and physical protective measures
- TO 3 — Utilize interdiction and disruption for prevention and protection (cyber and kinetic)
- TO 4 — Respond to cyber and kinetic attacks
- TO 5 — Develop plans for Civil Support of Military Operations (CSMO)
- TO 6 — Exercise TNSG augmenting TNNG forces for HD purposes

**EO 2 — Improve TNNG damage assessment support capabilities.**

- TO 1 — Utilize unmanned aerial reconnaissance
- TO 2 — Incorporate the Joint Bridge Assessment Team
- TO 3 — Respond with operational coordination and situational assessments
- TO 4 — Provide response support with critical transportation
- TO 5 — Provide response support with operational communications

**EO 3 — Rehearse the emergency communications plan.**

- TO 1 — Simulate a communications blackout
- TO 2 — Exercise TACN / LMR
- TO 3 — Exercise HF radio
- TO 4 — Exercise satellite phones
- TO 5 — Exercise aerial retransmission via aircraft

**EO 4 — Exercise the TERP (Tennessee Emergency Response Plan — All Hazards).**

- TO 1 — Activate and conduct the EMAC process
- TO 2 — Activate JABS (Joint Adaptive Battle Staff) for mission command in preparation for follow-on missions from TEMA

## Scenario

### Early Notice (published 4 MAY 26, kicks things off)

> "The State Emergency Operations Center (SEOC) is monitoring a developing weather system forecast to impact Tennessee beginning late this week and persisting into next week. Current model guidance indicates the potential for multiple rounds of heavy rainfall, severe thunderstorms, and widespread flooding from May 9–14. The overall pattern bears similarities to previous long-duration high-impact events, including May 2010 and August 2021. While confidence in exact timing and rainfall totals will increase in the coming days, early projections indicate 6–10 inches of rain statewide, with higher localized amounts possible, particularly in East and Northeast Tennessee."

**Primary hazards (early outlook):**

- Heavy rainfall / flash flooding — urban and small-stream flooding possible 9-10 MAY, transitioning to river flooding thereafter
- Severe thunderstorms — a squall line late 9-10 MAY may produce damaging winds and power outages
- Landslides — elevated risk in steep terrain, especially Northeast Tennessee (Greene, Washington, Hawkins)
- Infrastructure impacts — roadway washouts, culvert failures, power disruptions
- Extended-duration impacts — power restoration and access issues may persist beyond the rainfall window
