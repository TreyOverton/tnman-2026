## 2. Mission

> The Division will participate in TNMAN Operations **13-17 May 2026** to conduct Homeland Defense (HD) operations in response to ongoing attacks on the homeland, across TN. Improve TN Military Department damage assessment support capabilities, rehearse and train on emergency communications plans, in order to (IOT) improve and demonstrate TNSG functional capabilities and to exercise the TN Emergency Response Plan.

## 3. Execution

### Commander's Intent

> This order is to synchronize the efforts of all units throughout the Division IOT maintain and improve and demonstrate TNSG functional capabilities and exercise the TN Emergency Response Plan.

**Vision:** Officers gaining a better understanding of ICP establishment, JBAT policies and regulations, UAV/UAS Policy across State and Federal properties, and developing contacts from civilian emergency management counterparts at local levels. WO SMEs will increase Company Grade Officer, NCO, and enlisted expertise across Operational Concepts, troop leading procedures, pre-operational checks/inspections, and recognition of branches and sequels to "The Plan." TNMAN '26 provides an opportunity for local, state, and federal agencies, National Guard and State Guard Units, and private-sector partners to improve emergency response plans, inter-agency relationships, and interoperability.

**Key Tasks:**

1. Deploy safely to mission locations
2. Redeploy safely to home station
3. Zero Safety Incidents During TNMAN
4. 100% Accountability of all personnel and equipment
5. Conduct Command and Control at all levels
6. Conduct Sustainment Support Operations
7. Provide security of all equipment and personnel at all times
8. Identify equipment, transportation and personnel requirements early
9. Provide Risk Assessment

**End State:**

> The TNSG's **3rd Regiment's successful establishment of an Incident Command Post** for conducting C3 and SECFOR Mission supporting Holston Army Ammunitions Plant, the TNSG G6 Cyber security team conducting TTX to the TNARNG G6 and Oak Ridge National Laboratory and the Y12 National Security Complex, and the TNSG Division Engineer Detachment attached to the JBAT conducting Bridge Damage Assessment will demonstrate TNSG capabilities in a Homeland Defense Scenario.
