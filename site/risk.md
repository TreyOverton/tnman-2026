## Standing Mission Constraints (from CG and HAAP)

- **Dawn-to-dusk duty day** — Per CG direction, TNSG participation in TNMAN 26 is limited to daylight hours. No overnight assembly at the ICP.
- **RON at home or hotel** — Soldiers participating on more than one day must return home (or to a hotel of their choosing) overnight between duty days. Billeting at the ICP is not authorized.
- **Perimeter-only operations at HAAP** — No TNSG personnel are authorized to enter the actual premises of the Holston Army Ammunition Plant. All 3 RGT operations are conducted on the outside perimeter only. The ICP is likewise sited outside the HAAP perimeter.
- These constraints apply to all ranks and are baseline controls on every hazard below — not optional mitigations. They substantially reduce exposure to HAAP interior hazards (explosive overpressure zones, interior industrial traffic, hazmat inventories) and concentrate remaining operational risk on perimeter SECFOR, public-road adjacency, and weather.

## Overall Residual Risk: <span class="mrc mrc-2">MODERATE</span>

**Rationale:** After the current round of control adjustments, most lines sit at Low residual:

1. **HAAP interior hazards (O-2)** — removed by the perimeter-only + ICP-outside-perimeter constraint. O-2 now M/L.
2. **Severe weather (O-1)** — 3 RGT is authorized to call off participation entirely for forecast severe weather; "don't go" is a viable control for a training exercise. O-1 now H/L.
3. **Convoy ops (O-3)** — dawn-to-dusk driving only, no HAAP interior exposure, 2-driver rule for long legs, authorized hotel stays for long commutes. O-3 now H/L.
4. **SECFOR outdoor duty (O-4)** — TNSG is **unarmed by default and no Soldiers will carry weapons**, removing weapon handling and use-of-force concerns. O-4 now L/L.
5. **Comms blackout inject (O-6)** — PACE plan plus personal mobile devices as an independent out-of-band fallback. O-6 now M/L.
6. **Medical readiness M-2 / M-3** — **binary controls**: either deploy with a fully mitigated deviation or do not deploy at all. Both lines sit at L residual.

**Remaining Moderate residuals that drive the aggregate rating:**

- **O-7 Civilian interaction / ROE clarity** — SECFOR adjacent to public roads, fence-line observers, and TCP posture even without weapons keeps this at M residual pending JAG-approved written ROE.
- **L-1 Title 10 / SAD command authority seam** — M residual pending JAG consultation and documented command relationships.

**Approval authority:** With the overall residual at Moderate, approval authority is the mission commander — **COL Roark, 3 RGT CO**.

**Medical readiness deviation** (M-2, M-3) still requires separate concurrence from **BG Cox (DCG-O / 61st MED BN CO)** — see the [DD Form 2977](docs/dd2977.md) medical readiness addendum. This concurrence is independent of the overall residual risk rating and is required whenever any MRC-3 or MRC-4 Soldier is proposed for deployment.

**Re-evaluation triggers** — any of the following would cause a reassessment of the overall rating:

- A Soldier's status changes during the exercise (injury, loss of battle buddy coverage)
- Weather forecasts push O-1 above Moderate residual at H-72
- JAG consultation does not conclude before execution (L-1 would climb)
- A deviation is approved but the associated mitigations (single-day only, ICP-interior, etc.) cannot be enforced in practice
