### Background

At approximately **240200RAPR26** (24 APR, 0200R), Greeneville PD responded to a violent-disoriented-patient call at a downtown clinic on Main Street. Within 90 minutes, **six** similar calls came in across the city. By 0500R, dispatchers were taking calls of civilians attacking civilians. **Greene County Sheriff Hayden W. Kirkbride** declared a public-safety emergency at 240520RAPR26 and requested mutual-aid assistance through TEMA East Region.

By the time the IDT muster forms up the next morning, the situation has been ongoing for **~33 hours**. Affected individuals have been observed across an approximately **5-block area of downtown Greeneville** (Main St / Depot St / Church St / Andrew Johnson Hwy / Tusculum Blvd). The civilian count in the cordon zone is unknown but believed to be in the **low hundreds**. **~30 affected individuals confirmed** as of the morning muster.

- **Slow ambulatory speed** (3-5 mph max — a child on a bike outpaces them, per Greeneville PD reports)
- **Disoriented and aggressive** toward bystanders; appears to ignore non-bystander stimuli

## Form-by-Form Reference

Each section: what the form is, who fills it, the critical fields, the most common mistakes (so you avoid them), and sample fill-ins drawn from the scenario.

### ICS 214 — Activity Log *(everyone fills one)*

- **What it is:** Time-stamped log of activities, decisions, observations. Most foundational ICS form. **One per individual or unit per operational period.**
- **Who fills it:** *Every assigned individual.* Section Chiefs may also keep a unit-level 214.
- **Critical fields:** Incident Name, OP Period, Name + ICS Position, **Time + Notable Activities**, Resources Assigned (if unit-level), **Signature, Date/Time** at bottom.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Writing duties / responsibilities instead of **discrete time-stamped events**
  - Skipping the time column
  - Forgetting to sign and date at the end of the period
  - Editorializing — keep entries factual, neutral, terse
- **Sample entries** (use as inspiration, not verbatim):
  - 0900R — Received scenario brief at HHC Afton
  - 0935R — Comm check with Patrol BRAVO at NAI 2 (Main St / Depot St); 5x5
  - 1012R — Briefed by CPT Pemberton on shift change at 1300R
  - 1047R — Greeneville PD reports affected individual sighted at corner of Main St / Church St; relayed to Branch Director per SOP
- **When activity outpaces the pen:**
  - **If staffing allows, split the role** — one Soldier on the radio, one with the pen.
  - **If it doesn't, scratch-and-backfill** — keep a small notebook or scrap pad; during the surge, capture *time + 2-3 word stub* (e.g., *"0917 — PD Main+Church"*); during the next lull, transcribe the stubs into your 214 with full sentences. Don't try to compose prose mid-surge. **Timeliness over polish** — a messy entry made now beats a tidy entry that never happens.

### ICS 205 — Incident Radio Communications Plan

- **What it is:** The **single source of truth** for radio channels in use during the OP period — function, frequency / talkgroup, who uses, mode (analog / digital / mixed), tones.
- **Who fills it:** Communications Unit Leader (SFC Whitford in this scenario). Approved by Planning Section Chief.
- **Critical fields:** Channel #, **Function** (Command, Tactical, Air-Ground, etc.), Channel Name / Trunked Radio System Talkgroup, RX Freq + Narrow/Wide, RX Tone, TX Freq, TX Tone, Mode (A/D/M), Remarks.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Mixing up RX and TX columns
  - Leaving Function blank — without it, you have a freq list, not a comms *plan*
  - Forgetting tones (especially for analog repeaters)
  - Listing channels nobody is actually using on this incident
- **Channels for this exercise** *(fictional but realistic)*:
  - **CH 1 — NSG Command:** TACN Talkgroup 401-CMD, Digital, no tones
  - **CH 2 — NSG Patrol:** TACN Talkgroup 402-PTL, Digital
  - **CH 3 — Greeneville PD Dispatch:** 154.785 RX / 159.135 TX, Analog Wide, RX Tone 100.0, TX Tone 100.0
  - **CH 4 — Sheriff Tac:** 460.225 RX / 465.225 TX, Analog Wide, RX 156.7, TX 156.7
  - **CH 5 — EMS MED-7:** 463.000 RX / 468.000 TX, Analog Wide
  - **CH 6 — Fire TAC-1:** 154.355 simplex, Analog Wide
  - **CH 7 — Simplex Backup:** 142.150 simplex, Analog Narrow
  - **CH 8 — Air-Ground (MEDEVAC):** 123.025 simplex, Analog (aviation band)

### ICS 205A — Communications List

- **What it is:** A phonebook for the incident — every position with name, phone, email, alternate. Plain, no frills. *The easiest of the bunch.*
- **Who fills it:** Resource Unit Leader (or any planner).
- **Critical fields:** Position, Name, Method (cell / desk / sat / radio), Number, Email.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Listing only names without **positions** (when the person rotates out, the list is stale)
  - Missing rank / title
  - No alt contact (cell *and* desk for key positions)
  - Not flagging the IC and Section Chiefs clearly
- **Sources for the data:** Use the [Command Roster](#command-roster) and [Partner POCs](#interagency-partner-pocs) above. Produce a clean, sorted list with section grouping and a "**(IC)**" or "**(SC)**" tag on the principals.

### ICS 206 — Medical Plan

- **What it is:** Where injured personnel and bystanders go and how they get there. Includes hospitals, EMS staging, MEDEVAC, on-site aid stations, and emergency-procedure call-down.
- **Who fills it:** Medical Unit Leader (SSG Halliburton); approved by Safety Officer (CW3 Pickering).
- **Critical fields:** Aid Stations (location, contact), Transportation (ambulance staging, MEDEVAC), **Hospitals (name, address, phone, distance, capabilities — burn / trauma level / helipad Y/N)**, Emergency Procedures (the call-down — "for any injury, call X on Channel Y").
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Listing hospitals without **capabilities or distance** — useless to a medic in the moment
  - Forgetting helipad status — affects MEDEVAC routing
  - No call-down procedure — people freeze when injured
  - Forgetting MEDEVAC POC + ETA expectation
- **Sample fill-ins:**
  - **Aid Station:** HHC Afton, NSG medic on-site (SSG Halliburton)
  - **Hospital:** Greeneville Community Hospital East — 401 Takoma Ave, ~6 mi from HHC Afton, ED line 423-555-0091, Trauma III equiv, helipad: yes
  - **MEDEVAC:** Wings Air Rescue, 800-555-0464, ETA 18 min from request
  - **Bite-injury protocol** *(scenario-specific)*: any NSG bite-victim — withdraw, call EMS on CH 5, **30-min quarantine** before transport (per CDC field guidance, fictional). Transport vehicle marked + driver isolated.
  - **Emergency Procedures:** Any injury, call SFC Whitford on CH 1, who triggers EMS dispatch and MEDEVAC if criteria met.

### ICS 209 — Incident Status Summary

- **What it is:** The "big picture" briefing form. Snapshot of the incident at a moment in time. **Goes UP** — to the EOC, to TEMA, to higher HQ. *Summary, not log.*
- **Who fills it:** Planning Section Chief (CPT Pemberton).
- **Critical fields:** Incident Type, Location, Op Period, **Resources Committed** (count, type, agency), **Damage / Casualties**, Weather, **Outlook for next OP**, Public Info Issued, **Critical Resource Needs** (this is the request-for-help section).
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Treating it as a detailed log (that's the 214) — keep entries summary-level
  - Forgetting to identify next-period objectives
  - No critical resource needs listed → higher HQ assumes you don't need anything
  - No casualty count, even if the count is "zero NSG, two civilian"
- **Sample fill-ins:**
  - Incident Type: Civil Disturbance / Public Health Emergency
  - Location: Downtown Greeneville, TN (5-block cordon: Main / Depot / Church / Andrew Johnson Hwy / Tusculum Blvd)
  - Resources Committed: 4 × 5-PAX NSG patrols (unarmed observe-only); 12 × Greeneville PD; 8 × Sheriff; 4 × EMS units; 1 × Fire engine (staging)
  - Casualties: 0 NSG, 4 LE (1 critical bite — quarantined; 3 minor), 6 civilian unaccounted for
  - Weather: Clear, 68°F, low wind, no impact
  - Outlook: Cordon expected to hold through next OP. Containment goal H+18.
  - Critical Resource Needs: 6 additional 5-PAX SECFOR teams (unarmed), 2 sat-phones, 200 ration packs

### ICS 213 — General Message

- **What it is:** Formal message form for note-passing inside the ICP and to higher / partners. Carbon-copy preserves the conversation. **Used like email when there is no email.**
- **Who fills it:** Anyone. Routed by the Comms Unit.
- **Critical fields:** Incident Name, **To** (with title), **From** (with title), **Subject**, Date / Time, **Message** (body), **Reply** (filled by recipient), Signature.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Vague subject lines ("Update," "FYI" — useless when stacked with 30 others)
  - No date / time stamp
  - Mixing the Message and Reply blocks (Reply is for the *recipient*)
  - Multi-paragraph essays — keep it tight (5 sentences max usually)
- **Sample messages:**
  - **From:** CPT Pemberton (PSC, NSG 3 RGT) **To:** Dir. Renfro (TEMA East) **Subj:** RFF — 6 additional unarmed SECFOR teams, IOT extend cordon to Tusculum Blvd. Need by 251700R.
  - **From:** Patrol BRAVO **To:** SFC Whitford (NSG Comms UL) **Subj:** Civilian volunteer at outer cordon claiming "cure" — request guidance, am holding position.
  - **From:** SSG Halliburton (NSG Med UL) **To:** Wings Air Rescue **Subj:** Pre-coordination — bite-victim transport criteria, advise standoff protocol.


## Before You Turn In Your 214

Three things at the end — all on the same 214 — so we don't need separate sign-in sheets or capture forms:

1. **Your 214 is also the attendance sheet.** Make sure the top has your rank, name, and the ICS role you picked. Sign and date the bottom.
2. **Write any AAR comments on the back** — what worked for you, what didn't, what you'd want different next time, anything you noticed about the forms or the scenario. Keep it short; bullet points are fine.
3. **Note which forms you participated in.** Anywhere on the 214 (back is fine), jot a quick line for each non-214 form:
   - **Filled (P)** — you personally wrote on the form
   - **Observed (O)** — your group filled it but you watched / co-discussed
   - *(skip a form entirely if your group didn't get to it)*
   - Example: *"205A — P; 209 — O"*

Everything goes to the NCOIC at the end. They'll bundle and hand to the OIC. Three artifacts captured in one piece of paper.


## Wrap-Up Takeaways

1. **Where each form lives in the IAP:** 205, 205A, 206, 209 are part of the daily Incident Action Plan, signed by the Planning Section Chief. 214 is *kept by everyone* and turned in at end of OP. 213 is transactional and filed in the comms log.
2. **The 214 is the historical record.** If it isn't on a 214, it didn't happen — true for AAR, true for legal review, true for media.
3. **205 and 205A are different.** 205 is *radio*; 205A is *phone / email*. Both, every OP.
4. **209 goes UP.** Anything you want higher HQ or TEMA to see, put it on the 209.
5. **213 is for when email is broken or someone needs a paper trail.**
